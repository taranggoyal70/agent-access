# YC readiness scorecard

## Current verdict

Agent Access has a credible technical wedge and working vertical slice, but it is not a 10/10 YC application until real design partners repeatedly route staging traffic through it. Product completeness cannot substitute for demand.

## Score today

| Dimension | Score | Evidence required for 10/10 |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Founder velocity | 8/10 | Continue shipping weekly from observed partner blockers |
| Working product | 9/10 | One outside company completes onboarding without founder database intervention |
| Market timing | 8/10 | Three buyers confirm agent access is an active roadmap blocker |
| Differentiation | 6/10 | Partners choose the import-to-safe-runtime workflow over incumbent IAM or an MCP gateway |
| User evidence | 1/10 | Three active Design Partners and more than 100 weekly upstream executions |
| Retention | 0/10 | Two partners use the product in four consecutive weeks |
| Revenue or commitment | 0/10 | One paying customer or signed design-partner commitment |

## Brutal competitive truth

Agent identity is becoming crowded. Microsoft Entra, Okta, Teleport, credential platforms, MCP gateways, and emerging protocols all cover parts of identity, delegation, and governance. “Identity for agents” is not a defensible pitch by itself.

The sharper wedge is: **connect an existing SaaS API and turn it into a safe, self-serve product for third-party agents in one afternoon.** The integrated workflow—contract preflight, risk diff, staging connection, delegated account, read-only mediation, and signed redacted evidence—is the product. Generic IAM is an ingredient or future integration, not the whole competitor.

## Four-week founder plan

### Week 1: recruit and observe

- Contact 40 tightly matched SaaS founders or platform leads.
- Complete 10 calls around a real external-agent workflow.
- Ask every qualified company to import its real contract during the call.
- Do the integration work manually if necessary.

### Week 2: make one partner live

- Connect one staging API.
- Publish one useful read-only workflow.
- Have an agent outside Agent Access discover, register, and execute it.
- Record every onboarding failure and fix only repeated blockers.

### Week 3: prove repetition

- Reach three connected staging APIs.
- Drive 100 verified upstream executions in a week.
- Measure whether partners return without reminders.
- Ask directly what would make them pay now.

### Week 4: convert and apply

- Obtain one paid pilot or written design-partner commitment.
- Capture a 60-second real workflow demo.
- Apply to YC with exact users, executions, retention, and learning—not projected market size.

## Stop-doing list

- Stop adding unrelated console pages without observed demand.
- Stop counting friendly conversations, events, GitHub activity, or sample imports as traction.
- Stop pitching investors before the first partner repeatedly uses the product.
- Stop describing the company as a broad agent identity platform.
- Do not add production writes until read-only staging usage proves the trust model.

## Application sentence

Agent Access lets SaaS companies connect their existing API and safely serve external AI agents with self-serve onboarding, delegated access, and verifiable execution receipts.
